# HEMK User Validation - Recruitment Strategy
## Strategic User Testing for Investment Decision

**Document Type**: User Recruitment and Testing Strategy  
**Version**: 1.0  
**Date**: July 13, 2025  
**Author**: Senior User Experience Validation Specialist  
**Target Audience**: HEMK Project Manager, Testing Coordinators  

---

## Executive Summary

This recruitment strategy targets 5-8 traditional enterprise network engineers with minimal Kubernetes experience to validate the HEMK value proposition. The recruitment emphasizes authenticity over convenience, ensuring test participants genuinely represent the target market for the $1.2M-$1.8M investment decision.

### Recruitment Objectives

**Primary Goal**: Recruit diverse, representative sample of target users who can provide authentic validation data for strategic decision-making.

**Success Criteria**:
- 5-8 qualified participants recruited within 1 week
- Diverse experience levels and organizational contexts
- 100% participant completion rate for testing sessions
- High-quality feedback sufficient for confident go/no-go decision

---

## Target User Profile Specification

### Primary User Categories

#### 1. Traditional Enterprise Network Engineers (3-4 participants)
**Background Requirements**:
- 8-15 years enterprise networking experience (switching, routing, VLANs, BGP)
- Currently managing enterprise network infrastructure
- Minimal Kubernetes operational experience (<6 months)
- Familiarity with Git concepts but limited GitOps experience
- Comfortable with Linux command line operations

**Preferred Characteristics**:
- Currently evaluating or using Hedgehog technologies
- Experience with enterprise change management processes
- Preference for guided, step-by-step operational procedures
- Enterprise security and compliance awareness

#### 2. IT Infrastructure Generalists (2-3 participants)
**Background Requirements**:
- 5-12 years server/infrastructure management experience
- Responsible for multiple infrastructure technologies
- Some container/Docker experience but limited Kubernetes
- Experience with infrastructure automation tools
- Interest in GitOps concepts and implementation

**Preferred Characteristics**:
- Mid-size organizations with hybrid cloud environments
- Hands-on exploration learning style with documentation backup
- Experience with VMware, cloud platforms, automation tools
- Decision-making influence for infrastructure tools

#### 3. Hedgehog Evaluators (1-2 participants)
**Background Requirements**:
- Technical decision-making authority for network infrastructure
- Currently evaluating Hedgehog for organizational adoption
- Results-focused with emphasis on business value demonstration
- Willing to provide detailed business-focused feedback

**Preferred Characteristics**:
- Organizations considering network modernization
- Experience with vendor evaluation processes
- Strategic thinking about operational complexity reduction
- Influence on technology adoption decisions

### User Exclusion Criteria

**Participants NOT suitable for validation**:
- Kubernetes experts with >2 years operational experience
- DevOps engineers with extensive GitOps workflow experience
- Users without networking or infrastructure management background
- Participants not available for follow-up feedback and iteration
- Users with direct involvement in HEMK development

## Recruitment Process and Timeline

### Week 1: Recruitment Campaign Launch
**Days 1-2: Outreach Preparation**
- Finalize recruitment materials and messaging
- Establish tracking systems for participant management
- Brief recruitment team on target profile requirements
- Set up scheduling and communication infrastructure

**Days 3-5: Primary Outreach Campaign**
- Launch Hedgehog community outreach (highest priority)
- Initiate professional network engagement
- Begin partner organization coordination
- Start content marketing and social media engagement

**Days 6-7: Follow-up and Qualification**
- Follow up on initial outreach responses
- Conduct preliminary qualification calls
- Schedule testing sessions for qualified participants
- Continue recruitment for any remaining slots

### Week 2: Testing Session Execution
**Participant Confirmation and Preparation**
- Confirm all testing session participants and schedules
- Provide pre-session materials and environment access
- Brief participants on testing objectives and expectations
- Prepare testing environments and support resources

### Recruitment Qualification Process

#### Initial Contact and Screening
```
Qualification Call Structure (15 minutes):

1. Background Verification (5 minutes)
   - Current role and responsibilities
   - Years of networking experience
   - Kubernetes/container experience level
   - Organization type and infrastructure context

2. Availability and Interest Assessment (5 minutes)
   - Testing session availability
   - Interest level and motivation for participation
   - Previous experience with similar tools or approaches
   - Expectations and concerns about testing

3. Logistics and Next Steps (5 minutes)
   - Testing session format and duration explanation
   - Technical requirements and environment setup
   - Scheduling and communication preferences
   - Questions and concerns resolution
```

#### Participant Selection Criteria
**Must-Have Qualifications**:
- Meets primary user category requirements
- Available for complete testing session participation
- Willing to provide detailed feedback and follow-up
- Technical environment meets testing requirements

**Scoring Framework**:
```
Selection Scoring (1-5 scale):

- Experience level appropriateness: ______/5
- Organizational representativeness: ______/5
- Availability and commitment: ______/5
- Communication and feedback capability: ______/5

Minimum score for selection: 18/25
```

## Participant Management and Support

### Pre-Session Preparation

#### Participant Onboarding Package
```
Materials Provided 48 Hours Before Session:
1. Testing session agenda and expectations
2. Technical environment access credentials
3. Pre-session questionnaire (background and expectations)
4. Contact information for technical support
5. Session recording consent and confidentiality agreement
```

#### Pre-Session Technical Setup
```
Environment Validation Checklist:
- [ ] VM environment provisioned and accessible
- [ ] Network connectivity to required resources verified
- [ ] Screen sharing and recording software tested
- [ ] Backup communication channels established
- [ ] Observer access and note-taking systems prepared
```

### Session Support Infrastructure

#### Technical Support Team
**Roles and Responsibilities**:
- **Session Observer**: Primary note-taking and behavior analysis
- **Technical Support**: Environment troubleshooting and assistance
- **Facilitator**: Session guidance and feedback collection
- **Backup Support**: Secondary technical assistance if needed

#### Communication Protocols
```
Support Escalation Process:
1. Participant encounters technical issue
2. Facilitator provides initial guidance (max 2 minutes)
3. Technical support intervenes for environment issues
4. Session pause/restart if critical failure occurs
5. Alternative environment or schedule change if needed
```

### Post-Session Follow-up

#### Immediate Post-Session (Within 24 hours)
- Thank you message with session summary
- Preliminary feedback review and clarification questions
- Schedule follow-up interview if additional input needed
- Provide promised materials or information

#### Extended Follow-up (Within 1 week)
- Detailed feedback analysis and theme identification
- Follow-up questions on specific improvement suggestions
- Validation of any fixes or changes based on feedback
- Progress updates on overall validation results

## Risk Mitigation and Contingency Planning

### Recruitment Risks and Mitigation

#### Insufficient Response Rate (Medium Risk)
**Mitigation Strategy**:
- Multiple recruitment channels activated simultaneously
- Extended recruitment timeline if needed (up to 10 days)
- Partner organization collaboration for participant access
- Increased incentives or value proposition enhancement

#### Participant Quality Issues (Medium Risk)
**Mitigation Strategy**:
- Rigorous qualification process with scoring framework
- Backup participant recruitment (110% of target)
- Clear exclusion criteria and enforcement
- Pre-session validation calls to confirm suitability

#### Schedule Coordination Challenges (High Risk)
**Mitigation Strategy**:
- Flexible scheduling across multiple time zones
- Multiple session format options (in-person, remote)
- Buffer time built into testing schedule
- Alternative session arrangements for critical participants

### Testing Quality Risks

#### Technical Environment Issues (High Risk)
**Mitigation Strategy**:
- Multiple backup testing environments prepared
- Technical support team standing by for all sessions
- Pre-session environment testing and validation
- Alternative testing procedures for critical failures

#### Participant Dropout (Medium Risk)
**Mitigation Strategy**:
- Over-recruitment to account for expected dropout
- Flexible rescheduling policies for participant conflicts
- Strong participant engagement and communication
- Backup participants ready for immediate deployment
